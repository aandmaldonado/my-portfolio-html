About me

Who am I?

I hold a Bachelor's degree in Civil Engineering in Computer Science from the University of Santiago of Chile and a Master's degree in Artificial Intelligence from the Polytechnic University of Catalonia. With over a decade of professional experience, I have been actively involved in the development and implementation of computer solutions across diverse industries, including telecommunications, government entities, HR, banking, airlines, retail, AI/ML/DL and robotics. My expertise spans various phases of the software life cycle.


Throughout my career, I have worked both as a client and a service provider, in both large corporations and startups, assuming various roles such as Software Engineer, Solutions Analyst, IT Consultant, Technical Lead, and CTO.


I have honed a wide array of skills, including goal-driven perseverance, self-management, leadership, problem-solving, critical thinking, teamwork, exceptional customer service, and a high adaptability to diverse tasks, technologies, responsibilities, and team dynamics, consistently achieving assigned objectives.


Currently, I am actively seeking dynamic and challenging opportunities where I can leverage my knowledge in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, acquired through my recent studies. I am also eager to further develop my leadership and strategic management skills to contribute to the successful execution of technology-driven projects.


My skill set includes proficiency in cloud computing, Amazon Web Services (AWS), AI/ML/DL, classification models, image and video processing, image recognition, CNN, NLP, Python, backend development, APIs, Java, databases, and SQL.

Main Projects

To which I have contributed

  • Faculties engine

    Aug 2023 - Present


    Project for Banco de Chile, aimed specifically at the corporate sector. This project contemplates the development of a sophisticated engine designed to process PDF files from the 'Your Company in a Day' portal, submitted by companies. The key function of the engine is to meticulously extract and analyze the legal authorizations included in these documents. This allows the bank to offer personalized products and services to customers, taking advantage of this valuable information. This translates into significant improvements in the optimization of business processes and efficiency in decision making.

  • Time & Attendance

    Aug 2022 - Jul 2023


    The Corporate Assistance Management System, implemented across multiple countries and businesses within the Falabella group, aims to improve the employee experience while addressing contemporary workforce needs. It emphasizes remote clock-in methods to reduce reliance on biometric devices and provides essential data for managing performance indicators, attendance, and shift scheduling efficiently. The project's key objectives include legal compliance, data acquisition for optimized scheduling, insights into absenteeism and overtime, and the introduction of remote clock-in technology. Key performance indicators encompass employee satisfaction, overtime hours, absenteeism rates, disputed clock-in records, and time spent on monthly attendance reconciliation, all contributing to a more streamlined and digitally enhanced workforce management process.

  • AcuaMattic AI & Robotics (Prototype)

    Nov 2020 - Jul 2022


    This project aims to improve social well-being by developing a tool that simplifies the process of acquiring and responsibly setting up aquariums, catering to individuals with varying levels of expertise in the hobby. It involves a fish classifier model meticulously trained on an extensive dataset of images and videos featuring common ornamental fish species. This model provides highly personalized assistance by identifying the species in a user's aquarium and communicating with an in-tank robot to gather precise water parameter data. Subsequently, it offers tailored recommendations for optimizing these parameters, ultimately promoting aquarium maintenance and the overall well-being of the aquatic species within.

  • AcuaMattic Dataset

    Nov 2020 - Jul 2022


    The project aims to address the lack of specialized image banks for ornamental fish, which hinders research and technology development for their preservation and care. To overcome this challenge, the project proposes creating a diverse dataset of high-quality images and videos of ornamental fish through controlled environment recordings and advanced image processing techniques. This dataset, covering 30 different species, is crucial for training classification models, offering the advantage of eliminating the need for synthetic data generation and enhancing dataset quality and reliability, ultimately facilitating advancements in ornamental fish research and care.

  • AcuaMattic Chatbot

    Mar 2022 - Jul 2022


    Creation of a Virtual Assistant that functions as an expert advisor in the field of aquariums. It is based on extensive conversations with aquarium experts. This innovative solution is hosted in a Cloud Computing environment, specifically AWS. The chatbot employs conversational AI and seamlessly integrates various functionalities including sentiment analysis, text analysis, computer vision, search capabilities, database interaction, voice communication, and secure data storage.

  • JuezSW

    Mar 2022 - Jul 2022


    This project focuses on developing a specialized management system for insolvency administration, specifically aimed at assisting companies undergoing payment suspension. Its primary function is to streamline the creation and documentation of asset liquidation plans while ensuring strict adherence to legal regulations to facilitate efficient and legally compliant auctions. To achieve this, the project leverages automated data collection, transformation, and processing techniques on a documentary corpus related to settlement plans. The ultimate goal is to identify patterns and use them to train Artificial Intelligence models capable of autonomously generating these documents. These AI models are designed to comprehend and apply the latest bankruptcy legislation, thereby substantiating the formulation of liquidation plans for presentation before a judge.

  • Migration to AWS

    Nov 2020 - Feb 2022


    In summary, the company successfully migrated its operations to the Amazon Web Services (AWS) cloud, addressing scalability and data processing challenges while enabling the creation of datasets for artificial intelligence applications.   • S-Box (Thesis Project)

    Mar 2016 - Jun 2017


    Project S-Box represents a groundbreaking innovation in Affective Computing, harnessing the capabilities of Artificial Intelligence and Computer Vision to meticulously examine videos associated with instances of smiling. This project exemplifies the harmonious collaboration between these two technologies, pushing the boundaries of our comprehension of human emotions and interactions with technology. S-Box serves as an indispensable tool for researchers, scientists, and professionals seeking profound insights into emotional responses in controlled experimental settings. Its key features include AI-driven smile analysis, multimodal capture with Computer Vision, precise automatic synchronization, AI-enhanced smile filtering, intelligent sequence generation, and AI-enhanced advanced visualization, collectively enabling a comprehensive understanding of facial expressions and emotional reactions during user-computer interactions.

    This project involved migrating Movistar Chile's private client portal, utilizing advanced IBM WebSphere technology for infrastructure, front-end (portlets), and back-end (web services) development. This migration represents a significant leap in improving user experience and operational efficiency, enabling clients to access a wider array of services and information from their devices, thus minimizing the need for in-person interactions and manual procedures.
